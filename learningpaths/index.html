Structured versus Unstructured Learning

  • Why not both?
  • Get the best of both the worlds.
  • Start with unstructured learning and generate the structure as you move along.

Learning versus Teaching

  • Learning is an important part of teaching.
  • Teaching is an important part of learning.
  • The line between learning and teaching has blurred.
  • Learn as you teach, Teach as you learn.

Unschool

  • A lot of education has moved away from schools and universities.
  • Availability of content has reduced the dependency on class room teachings.
  • MOOCs(Massive Open Online Courses) have introduced the concept of nano degrees.
  • You can now become proficient in the specific topics you choose.

Relevance of resources available

  • A lot of publicly available content looses its relevance very quickly.
  • As an example tehcnology is growing at a very fast pace and most of the tutorials are not relevant within one year of their writing.
  • People shift their interests and preferences frequently and abandon their projects of content sharing.
  • There is no incentive to keep the tutorials uptodate.

Paid Walls

  • A lot of good learning resources are behind the paid walls.
  • Paid walls are invevitable today to safeguard the interests of content creators.
  • This model keeps lot of good content out of reach for most of the readers.

Enter Cryptocurrencies

  • Cryptocurrencies open up a new opportunity for safeguarding the interests of both content creators and consumers.
  • A token system that safeguards the interest of the all the stakeholders can keep the learning domain active.
  • There are existive cryptocurrency platforms which we can leverage to build a comminity that creates value.

Community

  • New model helps us put the power in the hands of the community. Right where it should belong.
  • Content creation, Content curation and content distribution forms three pillars of Knowledge dissemination.
  • This model ensures to involve the community in all these three and let the community decide the value of each contribution.

Learning Paths

  • A platform to convert rabbit holes into learning paths.
  • The platform is primarily made of Learning Paths, Learning Pointers and Learning Resources

Terminology

  • Rabbit holes
  • Learning Buckets
  • Learning Paths
  • Learning Pointers
  • Learning Resources

Rabbit holes

  • Rabbit hole is a metaphor for an entry into the unknown.
  • Rabbit holes are the beginning points for many learning adventures.
  • Rabbit holes have also led to many discoveries which were not the end goal.
  • Read our blog The thing about Rabbit holes to know more about why we think entering rabbit holes form the crux of learning.

Learning Buckets

  • A high level collection for organizing all your activity across internet.
  • Rabbit holes are the beginning points for many learning adventures.

Learning Buckets Example

  • Blockchain, Javascript, Drupal are examples of Learning Buckets

Learning Paths

  • Learning Paths are rabbit holes that have a structure and an outcome assigned to them.
  • Ideally every rabbit hole of your interest should transition to a learning path as you spend more time on it.
  • Since that is not the case in general, we have decided to do our part in making it easier for you.

Learning Paths Example

  • "How to start coding with blockchains?" is an example for Learning Path.

Learning Pointers

  • Every learning path is made up of Learning Pointers.
  • If you can compare your area of interest to a learning path, Learning pointer refers to every topic or subtopic that you would conver under the particular learning path.
  • Every learning path is made up of Learning Pointers.

Learning Pointers Example

  • Considering "How to start coding with blockchains?" as a learning path we can say "What is blockchain?", "Technical architecture of a blockchain" and "Programming languages you need to know to code on blockchain" are examples of Learnign Pointers.

Learning Resources

  • Every learning resource that adds to your understanding of a learning pointer/topic is a learning resources
  • So every blog you read or a video you see is an example of learning resource.

Learning Resources Example

  • For the learning pointer "What is blockchain?", the blog What is blockchain technology and the video Understand what is blockchain in two minutes are examples of Learning Resources.
